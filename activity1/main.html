Avengers: Infinity War (2018)


Overview

Avengers: Infinity War (2018) is a Marvel superhero film that follows the Avengers and their allies as they battle against the powerful villain Thanos. Thanos seeks to collect all six Infinity Stones, which will grant him the power to eliminate half of all life in the universe with a snap of his fingers. The Avengers, along with the Guardians of the Galaxy and other heroes, try to stop him across various locations, including Earth and space.


Directors

Joe and Anthony Russo

Their breakthrough in Hollywood came when Marvel Studios tapped them to direct Captain America: The Winter Soldier (2014). The film was a critical and commercial success, known for its mix of action and political thriller elements. They followed it up with Captain America: Civil War (2016), which introduced a major rift between the Avengers and became one of the MCU's most important films.
After their success with the Captain America films, the Russo brothers were given the helm of the two-part Avengers saga: Infinity War (2018) and Endgame (2019). These films became massive global hits, with Endgame becoming one of the highest-grossing films of all time.
