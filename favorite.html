The Chronicle of Narnia

The Chronical of Narnia is a series of seven fantasies authored by C.S. Lewis. It is about the adventure of the 4 children in a magical land called Narnia. This series shows the struggle between good and evil, represented by the noble lion Aslan and the White Witch.


Main Characters

  • Aslan: The great lion that serves as the figure of Narnia and symbolizes good and justice. Aslan is powerful and wise, guiding the characters in their journey and the fate of Narn
  • Peter Pevensie: The eldest of the Pevensie siblings. Peter is courageous, noble, and a natural leader. He becomes the High King of Narnia and plays a key role in the battles against evil.
  • Susan Pevensie: The second eldest Pevensie sibling, Susan is practical, caring, and protective of her younger siblings. She becomes Queen Susan the Gentle and is known for her wisdom and diplomacy.
  • Edmund Pevensie: The third Pevensie sibling, Edmund initially struggles with jealousy and selfishness but undergoes significant character development. He eventually becomes a just and brave king, known as Edmund the Just.
  • Lucy Pevensie: The youngest Pevensie sibling, Lucy is kind-hearted, brave, and perceptive. She is the first to discover Narnia and often has a special connection with Aslan. She becomes Queen Lucy the Valiant.
  • Prince Caspian: The rightful heir to the Narnian throne, Caspian is a courageous and noble character who seeks to reclaim his kingdom from the usurping King Miraz.
  • White Witch (Queen Jadis): The primary antagonist in The Lion, the Witch and the Wardrobe. She rules Narnia with an icy grip, bringing eternal winter and opposing Aslan. She embodies tyranny and evil.

Movie Setting

Narnia is magical land beyond the wardrobe where its realm is caught by winter, and is ruled by the White Witch. In Narnia, talking animals is free to roam, and mythical creatures are common.


The landscape is a mesmerizing tapestry of icy beauty: tall pine trees laden with snow, frozen rivers glinting under the pale sunlight, and mountains that loom like silent sentinels.


The heart of Narnia lies in the great stone castle of Cair Paravel, where legends are born and destinies are forged. It is a place of ancient magic and hidden power, where the forces of good and evil are in constant tension


Plot of the Movie

  • Arrival in Narnia

    • The four sibling - Peter, Susan, Edmund, and Lucy - was sent to the countryside after the World War II to stay with an eccentric professor. While roaming around the vast and house of the professor, Lucy discovered a magical wardrobe that serves as the portal to the land of Narnia.

  • The White Witche's Reign

    • Narnia was once under the rule of White Witch who cursed the land to frost. Her reign is characterized by fear and oppression and the inhabitants of Narnia is living in hiding while waiting for the return of the prophecy that promises to end the Witch's tyranny.

  • The Propechy and the Return of Kings and Queens

    • According to an ancient prophecy, when two sons of Adam and two daughters of Eve sit on the thrones of Cair Paravel, the White Witch’s rule will end, and Narnia will be restored. The Pevensie siblings, who are the prophesied children, gradually find their roles in this grand destiny.

  • Edmund's Betrayal and Redemption

    • Edmund betrays his siblings under the influence of the White Witch, who promises him power and Turkish Delight. However, he soon realizes the true nature of her evil and is rescued by Aslan, the great lion who represents good and the rightful king of Narnia.

  • Aslan's Sacrifice and the Battle

    • Aslan, who is a symbol of hope and justice, sacrifices himself in Edmund’s place to save him from the Witch's wrath. His selfless act of bravery enables the prophecy to unfold. Aslan is resurrected, symbolizing the return of warmth and renewal to Narnia.

  • The Restoration of Narnia

    • With Aslan's return, the Witch’s forces are defeated in a climactic battle. The Pevensie siblings are crowned as the new kings and queens of Narnia. Under their benevolent rule, the land flourishes once again, emerging from its icy prison into a vibrant and joyous place.

  • The End of the Adventure

    • After many years of peace and prosperity, the Pevensies eventually return to their world, leaving Narnia to its new golden age. Their departure marks the end of their reign, but the legacy of their adventure and the triumph of good over evil endure in the hearts of Narnians.
